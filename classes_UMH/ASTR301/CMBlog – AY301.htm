(JT) this is colmerge, also known as cm. Here's a worked example, explanations available as desired:

Dump out all the log files to find out what observations overlap a point of interest. Note that these log files are not always accurate, and should (will) be regenerated. 

  for m in {57452..57462} ; do
    awk '$5==20 && $8<3 && $9>500 && $10>15 && $11!=0 && $12!=0{print $0}' /atlas/red/01p/$m/$m.log
  done > /tmp/all.log

Pick a point of interest and a radius, and identify all the observations that overlap the point to within the radius: 

  RA0=90 DEC0=0 RAD=1
  echo $RA0 $DEC0 | cm 2,1 - 12,11 /tmp/all.log -tol $RAD,d | awk '{print $4}' > /tmp/list.$RA0.$DEC0

Dump all the stars from all the fields that overlap the point of interest: 

  rm /tmp/hunk.$RA0.$DEC0
  for f in $(cat /tmp/list.$RA0.$DEC0) ; do
    m=$(echo $f | tr '[a-z]' ' ' | awk '{print $2}')
    obs=$(echo $f | awk '{print substr($1,1,14)}')
    dev=$(echo $f | awk '{print substr($1,15,1)}')
    echo $obs $dev
    awk -v obs=$obs -v dev=$dev 'NR>1{printf "%s %d %s\n", obs, dev, $0}' /atlas/red/01p/$m/$f.dph >> /tmp/hunk.$RA0.$DEC0
  done

Find all the "friends-of-friends" groups among the stars we dumped out 

  time cm 4,3 /tmp/hunk.$RA0.$DEC0 -fof -tol 0.001,d -grp > /tmp/hunk.grp

Pick out a subset of groups that have at least 10 detections (i.e. probably good overlap within our desired center point and radius). 

  awk -v nmin=10 '$4>nmin{printf "%8.4f %8.4f\n", $8,$5}' /tmp/hunk.grp > /tmp/hunk.star

There are 126754 groups, and 55542 stars. Dump out the light curves: 

  time cm 2,1 /tmp/hunk.star 4,3 /tmp/hunk.$RA0.$DEC0 -tol 0.001,d > /tmp/hunk.lc
